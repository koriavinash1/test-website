Customer Needs

What does our customer want?

1> A small-sized power generator capable of powering everyday appliances.
2> Noiseless, eco-friendly power generator.
3> A generator (which can be used as a backup source)for charging Laptops, tablets, smartphones and other electrical devices when electric power supply is down.

Existing Solutions

1> Solar powered generators can only harness energy during daytime.
2> Also the solar generators have very low efficiency.
3> The photovoltaic cells used in these generators have an efficiency of around 15-20%.
4> Diesel engine generators are bulky, create a lot of noise and unwanted residue.
5> Harnesses energy through internal combustion.
6> Efficiency around 45%.

Technical Details Of Our Solution:

1> Aiming to build the generator which provides the power of 500 Watt, useful for charging laptops, tablets, smartphones and other domestic electrical devices and simultaneously running a fan and 2 tube-lights.
2> Planning to use dual cylinder beta-type stirling engine with inner fluid as fluorinated alkanes, ethers(due to their high molecular mass or lower boiling point property).
3> To reduce noise and for pollution control we are planning to use propyl-alcohol as fuel.

Calculations

From Fig.1 equation for displacer $$ {d_{1}} = {r_{1}}(1-cos\phi) + {0.5{{r_{1}^{2}(sin\phi)^{2}}}\over{{l_{1}}}}, {V_{exp}} = {d_{1}}A ...( A: Area) $$

From Fig.1 equation for power piston $$ {d_{2}} = {r_{2}}(1-cos(\phi-\theta)) + {0.5{{r_{2}^{2}(sin(\phi-\theta))^{2}}}\over{{l_{2}}}}, {V_{con}} = {d_{2}}A ...( A: Area) $$

From first law of thermodynamics total heat given to the system is equal to the sum heat given by the system and work done by the system.

From Fig.2 $$ {Q_{in}} = {Q_{cond}} + {Q_{conv}} + {W_{out}} $$

$$ \Rightarrow {Q_{in}} = {{kA\Delta \Theta}\over{\Delta t}} + {h2\pi{r}\int_{0}^{d_{1}}dx({T_{x} - T_{{\infty}1}})} + {W_{out}} $$

since Isothermal assumption$$ {T_{x}} = {T_{1}}$$

$$ \Rightarrow {Q_{in}} = {{kA({T_{heat}} - {T_{1}}})\over{\Delta t}} + {h2\pi{r}\int_{0}^{d_{1}}dx({T_{1} - T_{{\infty}1}})} + {W_{out}} $$


$$ \Rightarrow {T_{1}} = {{Q_{in}} - {kAT_{heat}\over {\Delta t}} + 2\pi{rd_{1}hT_{{\infty}1}} \over {{2\pi{rhd_{1}} + {nRAd_{1} \over V_{exp}} - {kA \over \Delta t}}}} $$

$$ {W_{in}} = {Q_{conv}} $$

$$ \Rightarrow {W_{in}} = {h2\pi{r} (T_{2} - T_{{\infty}2})\int_{0}^{d_{2}}dx} $$


$$ \Rightarrow {T_{2}} = {{2\pi{rhd_{2}T_{{\infty}2}}} \over {2\pi{rhd_{2} - {nRAd_{2} \over {V_{com} - A_{shaft}}}}}} $$

$$ P_{max} = \tau \omega $$

$$ \tau = F_{1}cos{\theta_{1}}r_{1} - F_{2}cos{\theta_{2}}r_{2} $$

$$ \Rightarrow \tau = p_{1}Acos{\theta_{1}}r_{1} - p_{2}(A-A_{shaft})cos{\theta_{2}}r_{2} $$

$$ \Rightarrow P_{max} = (p_{1}Acos{\theta_{1}}r_{1} - p_{2}(A-A_{shaft})cos{\theta_{2}}r_{2})\omega $$

$$ \Rightarrow P_{max} = {nR\omega \over \sqrt{2}}({{r_{1}{Q_{in} \Delta t - kAT_{heat} + 2\pi{rd_{1}hT_{{\infty}1}}\Delta t }\over {2\pi{rd_{1}h\Delta t} + nR\Delta t - kA}} - {{2\pi{rr_{2}hT_{{\infty}2}}} \over {2\pi{rd_{2}h} - nR}}}) $$

Geometric analysis: assuming some std. geometric relations for optimum output we get,

$$ l_{d} D_{D}^{2} = 1.5l_{p}D_{p}^{2} $$

Matlab Code

%% Geometric paramenters....
%lengths in m
l_cylinder = [11.79556: 0.00001: 11.79557];
thickness = .005;
Area = 0.101 .* l_cylinder .* l_cylinder;
r = sqrt(Area / pi);

shaft_area = 0.5;

%% crank lenghs
r_disp = 0.165 .* l_cylinder;
r_piston = 0.32 .* l_cylinder;

l_disp = 0.835 .* l_cylinder;
l_piston = 0.68 .* l_cylinder;

theta = 90; %phase difference
phi = 45;

% max. volume
%Vmax = displacer(r_disp, l_disp, 0) * Area + displacer(r_disp, l_disp, - theta * pi/180) * Area;

%% kinematic parameters....
%displacement of piston rod
d_disp = r_disp .* (1 - cos(phi .* pi/180)) + 0.5 .* (r_disp/l_disp) .* r_disp .* (sin(phi .* pi/180) .* sin(phi .* pi/180));
d_piston = r_piston .* (1 - cos((phi - theta) .* (pi/180))) + 0.5 .* (r_piston/l_piston) .* r_piston .* (sin((phi - theta) .* (pi/180)) .* sin((phi - theta) .* (pi/180)));

w = 800; % angular velocity
%% Thermodynamic parameters..............
n = 100; % number of moles...
R = 8.237; % gas constant
h = 40; % convection coefficient
k = 140; % thermal conductivity of cylinder
Qin = 420;
Theat = 900; % experession in terms of Qin
Troom = 300; % kelvin
Tconden = 250; % kelvin

%%calculations

pow = n .* R .* w .* ((r_disp .* cos(45 .* pi./180) ./ d_disp).*((Qin*thickness - k*Area*Theat + 2.*pi.*r.*d_disp.*h.*Troom.*thickness)./(2.*pi.*r.*d_disp.*h.*thickness + n.*R.*thickness - k.*Area)) - ((r_piston .* cos(45 .* pi ./ 180)).* ((2.*pi.*r.*h.*Tconden)./(h.*2.*pi.*r.*d_piston - n.*R) )));
% print value

%% plots
plot(l_cylinder, 100, '*',l_cylinder, pow)
% hold on
% plot(l_cylinder, 100, '.')
title('Graph of lcylinder and power for lcylinder Between 0 and 1 in m.')
xlabel('lcylinder') % x-axis label
ylabel('power') % y-axis label
